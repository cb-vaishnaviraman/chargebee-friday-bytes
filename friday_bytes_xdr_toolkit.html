Best practices for sellers — from Salesblazer

🎯 Build your buyer persona first. Know role, company, motivations & challenges before your first outreach.
🔍 Know your product like a power user. Test it yourself, ask customers daily — you can't sell what you can't explain.
📊 Use your CRM — actually. Log every interaction as it happens. It's your deal memory and forecast insurance.
🔥 Prioritise leads by conversion likelihood. Let data guide your time, not gut feel.
🤝 Lead with discovery, not demo. Ask great questions first. Trust is built before the pitch.
📖 Use customer stories as proof. A relevant case study beats any feature slide, every time.
⚡ Mine your past calls for gold. Insights from prior conversations tell you how to move the next deal forward.

How to work a cadence

1️⃣ Enrol prospects — confirm the right cadence for their stage first.
2️⃣ Personalise before you send — 20% personalisation = 2× reply rate.
3️⃣ Multi-channel it — red = auto-fires, green = your action. Email + call + LinkedIn = 77–91% more replies.
4️⃣ Hot lead? Drop everything — run the 3-step Call Now cadence immediately.
5️⃣ Final "Other" step — decide: nurture, re-enrol, or remove. Never ghost a prospect.

Salesforce sync — auto vs manual

✅ Auto-syncs: emails sent, calls logged, cadence steps — as long as the SFDC record exists.
✏️ Log manually: call outcomes/notes & meeting next steps. Log before you move on.
⚠️ Gaps that break sync: no SFDC record, personal email, skipped disposition, unfilled dynamic fields.

Quick workflow

▶ Start in Orum → select cadence or one-off tasks → review prospect context before dialing.
▶ Orum auto-advances through your list — skip when needed.
▶ Log call disposition + sentiment every time — keeps SFDC reporting clean.
▶ Calls write back to Salesloft (note includes "Orum" to distinguish from SL Dialer calls).
⚠️ Orum calls won't show in SL Calls section — use Call Reports for full volume.
